What is ISEEK?

The Institute for Social Empowerment through Entrepreneurship and Knowledge (ISEEK) was established at Purdue University’s Krannert School of Management in 2012 with a mission to empower the under-privileged and poverty-stricken population of the world. ISEEK is implementing a scalable, repeatable and sustainable intervention model using information and communication technology (ICT) with a goal of empowering the world’s disadvantaged youth, especially young women, for raising themselves out of poverty. ISEEK intensively collaborates with the Health and Human Sciences, Computer Science, Agriculture Technology, Global and Industrial Engineering, Liberal Arts, Visual Arts and International Management programs at Purdue.


About Us

To reach and engage this population segment will require: a clear road to empowerment; taking entrepreneurship and health education to the people through the creative use of mobile gaming technology; and a proactive intervention model that allows the participant to apply the gained knowledge in successfully starting and running a small business. ISEEK addresses these needs for empowerment by arming its participants with access to knowledge, capital, the market, and talent.



ISEEK takes education to people with the help of the mentor network and the use of mobile games. Through an increased understanding of vocational skills, entrepreneurship and personal health knowledge, ISEEK participants have the confidence, self-efficacy, and trust to start and sustain a business with a sense of empowerment rather than dependency.

ISEEK facilitates the flow of capital from various stakeholders (government, banks, investors, and philanthropists), thereby allowing investments for the small business to launch and grow. Further, Saksham growth fund is setup to help successful businesses to become formal enterprises, grow and compete in the global markets.

ISEEK is a market facilitator that provides a means for businesses to have a fair chance to succeed in the local and global markets. By linking the businesses to markets through ISEEK partners, these businesses can generate sustained revenues and thus lead better, more prosperous lives.

An enabling ecosystem of talented and dedicated individuals, groups, and organizations work with the target groups until they become self-reliant. ISEEK partners with individuals, NGOs and universities to help create the critical linkages that connect entities in the ecosystem.

SAKSHAM

Project Saksham (empowered in Hindi) is ISEEK's first international project. The project is based in the state of Jharkhand in India, where a large segment of the population lives below the subsistence level.

How ISEEK helps empower

Three billion people, primarily in the most depressed regions of the world, have been deprived of the benefits of the digital age. ISEEK aims to empower the parts of the population who have been largely forgotten and are the most difficult to reach: Youth aged 16-25, who have dropped out of school and are at risk of being trafficked, exploited, and living a life of poverty. The target population in each of ISEEK’s implementation countries depends on the context and the primary causes of poverty in the region.

In India’s state of Jharkhand, for example, ISEEK is targeting the 550,000 women between the ages of 16 and 25 who live under the subsistence level and are either illiterate or have dropped out of school before completing the 4th grade. In Bulgaria, ISEEK is targeting the Roma population, a marginalized community where the average age of marriage is 15. Alleviating poverty from this target group requires a clear road to empowerment, the deployment of a sustainable, customizable, repeatable and scalable information and communication technology solution, and an intervention model that translates gained knowledge into practical applications.

Our production- and knowledge-driven approach to development will integrate this population into the global economy. The resultant prosperity and growth will lead to new markets and demands. Our partners are the drivers and catalysts for change. Local NGOs and corporate partners bring extensive in-country knowledge and familiarity with the actors in the sector. ISEEK provides technology and thus monitoring, knowledge of the global market, and a robust package of entrepreneurship, health, and vocational training. By working with renowned scholars from Purdue University and beyond, as well as practitioners from large and small businesses worldwide, ISEEK has developed an ICT model that allows for iterative monitoring and incorporates concepts reflecting market trends and appropriate entrepreneurship knowledge.
