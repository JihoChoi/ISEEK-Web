Entrepreneurship

ISEEK’s vision to empower the disadvantaged population of the world is currently being served through its flagship project - “Saksham”. Project Saksham fosters grassroots entrepreneurship by providing access to knowledge, capital, talent, the market, and advanced technology through ISEEK’s ICT platform: “Daksh”.

The first phase of Project Saksham is being launched in the rural Indian state of Jharkhand in collaboration with the Government of India and the Wadhwani Foundation (https://www.wfglobal.org/). With a mission to empower the youth of India (especially women) by providing technology-enabled entrepreneurship education and on-the-job training, Project Saksham’s India program aims to facilitate the creation of over thousands of new companies, thereby employing over 100,000 business owners and reaching over 500,000 people.

Project Saksham is unique because it represents a holistic ecosystem seldom seen in development: it is neither a microfinance or education project alone. Rather, it arms its participants with access to four interdependent resources:




Project Saksham

ISEEK aspires to provide business and personal health training to Saksham participants through its app-based learning platform while linking social entrepreneurs with local non-governmental organizations (NGOs), universities and investors. ISEEK is a “market facilitator” through its e-commerce platform, thus using technology to create more jobs.



Project Saksham (empowered in Hindi) is ISEEK's flagship project . The project is based in the state of Jharkhand in India, where a large segment of the population lives below the subsistence level. Project Saksham will serve as a brand and a channel through which those associated with ISEEK will be able to sell and market the products they make - supporting their craft and providing them with continual business.


DEVELOPING AND GUIDING A GENERATION

Millions are kept from their potential due to lack of resources and social obstacles. The majority of those kept from achievement have the skills and resilience to be successful. ISEEK aims to help the world population achieve their potential and live their dreams.


OPENING CHANNELS FOR SUCCESS

ISEEK focuses on inclusive growth to help women gain access to a total ecosystem: business knowledge, micro-financing, and an avenue through which they can sell their products. By providing access to critical resources, Project Saksham will enable the women to profit from the talent, intuition, and ambition they already possess.

HELPING WOMEN PROFIT FROM THEIR SKILLS

We have begun working with illiterate women in Jharkhand, India from age 16 to 19 - none formally educated beyond 4th grade. In Jharkand, a multitude of products are made by skilled women using their local resource, Tasar silk.


Through increased understanding of vocational skills, how to run a business, and manage their health, ISEEK participants will be able to start and maintain a business with a mentality of empowerment rather than dependency.

ISEEK facilitates the flow of capital from various stakeholders (government, banks, and investors), thereby allowing investments for the small business to grow. This growth of investment is designed to pull the budding entrepreneurs and indirectly, the population affected by the new businesses, out of the poverty trap.

ISEEK is a market facilitator, meaning that it provides a means for businesses to have a fair chance for competition in the local and global marketplace. ISEEK entrepreneurs are creating debt in starting their business, and without a strong market, they would become worse off. By linking the new businesses to markets with which ISEEK has partnered, the businesses can make revenue. The ISEEK program focuses on 50% capacity of a group of people. The remaining capacity of young, business-minded individuals should be dedicated to exploring their own creativity in order to grow. Once a business is started and running well, its members will have the freedom to take innovative entrepreneurial risks.

ISEEK aims to build and ecosystem of talented and dedicated individuals, groups, and organizations that work with target groups until they become self-reliant. ISEEK partners with NGOs to help create the critical linkages that connect all entities in the ecosystem. Teams led by local universities ensure that the participants learn quality control concepts and tools that enable them to produce outputs at global standards.


Saksham Learn

This is an entrepreneurship and self-care game that will launch in the Google Play store in December 2016. ISEEK aims to reach more than 100,000 users in India by mid-2017. Features include:

* Interactive, goal-oriented, and visually engaging learning.

Daksh: Learning, Managing, and Market Facilitation Platform

Market facilitation is integral to Saksham’s success. Economic opportunity is not just the result of individual skills, but also of market efficiency. Inefficient markets produce less economic value for all participants than efficient markets. In some cases, these inefficiencies even lead to complete market failure. Market facilitation is a type of market intervention that works to stimulate markets while still remaining outside of those markets. Market facilitation targets relationships, ownership, and incentives. Because the market facilitator remains outside the market, the intervention is light-touch, and because the facilitator seeks to stimulate markets, it has a continuous exit strategy.

Resilient interventions require a large market, low transaction costs, and an efficient exchange of information between seller and buyer. Saksham accomplishes this with an online education, business, tradin and monitoring platform, “Daksh” (“expert” in Hindi). Daksh is Project Saksham’s integrated, cloud-based platform that makes market facilitation and intervention possible at scale. Daksh has four core features that are delivered through “gamified” smartphone apps: (1) Daksh Learning and Management Environment delivers business, health, and other related education and management content in the form of mobile games and apps. (2) Daksh e-marketplace links entrepreneurs to goods, services, capital, and knowledge markets. (3) Daksh Analytics uses state-of-the-art analysis techniques to continuously monitor performance and contributions of all stakeholders in the Saksham ecosystem and recommend proactive intervention. (4) Finally, Daksh Dashboards give stakeholders, e.g., banks, healthcare providers, government officials, access to insightful data about Saksham entrepreneurs and businesses.

Daksh forms the necessary global resource network to provide five access points for capacity building: knowledge, capital, the market, talent, and advanced technology.



Saksham brings education to people with the help of its mentor network and the use of mobile games. Through an understanding of vocational skills, entrepreneurship, and personal health, Saksham participants have the self-efficacy to start and sustain a business.

Saksham facilitates the flow of capital from various stakeholders to Saksham entrepreneurs, e.g. loans from a network of community banks and equity from Mentor Investors.

Daksh is a market facilitator that provides businesses a fair chance to succeed in local/global markets. By linking the businesses to markets through Saksham partners, the businesses can generate sustained revenues

An ecosystem of talented and dedicated individuals, groups, and organizations work with target groups until they become self-reliant. Saksham collaborates with these groups to create critical linkages and make them available to companies through Daksh.

Daksh is the centerpiece of Saksham’s Factory on Wheels advanced manufacturing model, in which the production of high end products is shared.

The Daksh platform is linked to multiple social media outlets like Facebook and Twitter. The advantage of linking Daksh to social media is that game components, course elements, and funding can all be globally crowd sourced. The online community is encouraged to take on roles of customizing, configuring, training, and mentoring the learner population.
